**Welcome to Your Assessment**

Thank you for your interest in joining our team. Before we begin, here are a few things to help you make the most of this conversation.

**What to expect**

You'll be chatting with an interviewer who will ask you about your past experiences. There are 5 main questions, and the conversation typically takes around 60 minutes. Take your time — there's no rush.

**How to give your best answers**

Be proud of your experience — whatever it looks like. Whether you've been working for ten years or you're just getting started, your story is worth telling in full.

When you think of an example, take us inside the moment. What was the situation? What did you actually do? How did it play out? The small details are what bring your answer to life — the tools you used, the steps you took, the people involved. Those things might seem ordinary to you, but they help us see the real you.

Your own words are your best asset. You don't need to prepare a perfect answer or follow any specific format — just share your experience the way it happened. The most memorable answers are always the ones that feel personal and real.

Don't skip the hard parts. Everyone faces challenges, makes mistakes, and has areas they're still growing in. Sharing those moments honestly shows self-awareness and maturity — and we value that just as much as success stories. If something didn't go perfectly, that's worth talking about too.

Give yourself credit for the full picture. If you led something, tell us the scope. If you built something, walk us through how. If you figured something out on your own, take us through the journey — including the dead ends. Your process matters just as much as the result.

**A few practical things**

Longer, detailed answers help us understand you better than short ones — so don't feel like you need to keep things brief. Share as much as feels right.

If you can't think of a work example, feel free to draw from school, personal projects, volunteer work, or anything else from your life. All experience counts.

If you need a moment to think, take it. A thoughtful answer that comes from a real memory is always worth the wait.

**Estimated time: ~60 minutes**
