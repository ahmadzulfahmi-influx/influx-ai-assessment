Why We Need This

We have AI grading — but static forms with fixed probes that can't adapt to what candidates actually say.

Current Approach

Before
  • Typeform — 5 question versions (V1–V5) randomized per candidate
  • AI-graded: Strong Yes (5) → Strong No (1) against recruiter benchmarks
  • Follow-up probes triggered if score < 3.5 — but fixed, pre-written
  • Probes can't adapt to what the candidate actually just said
  • Signal detection and quality check exist — but applied to static text
  • No conversational depth — candidates know it's a form, not an interview

After

  • AI generates probes based on what the candidate just said
  • 18 signal fields checked per answer — per candidate response
  • Same rubric every time — human-benchmarked, not rule-based

Two-Layer Scoring

Layer 1 — Field State Check
FilledCounts toward score; quality set by Layer 2
PartialCaps trait at score 3 unless upgraded by probe
EmptyPulls score down; scored as absent after probing
NegativeCaps trait at 2 max (1 if multiple negatives)
Layer 2 — Quality Descriptor
Score 5All fields filled, intentional & specific — goes beyond the bar
Score 4All fields filled, clear & honest — meets standard
Score 3Mix of filled & partial — generic or scripted, no depth
Score 2Mostly empty after probing — vague, minimal effort
Score 1Negative fills present — answer contradicts the trait

Final score per trait = field state pattern + quality level combined via per-slot scoring matrix

How This Was Scored — Prompt Engineering
Prompt A

AI Interviewer

Conducts the chat. Generates behavioral questions from structured templates (Q1–Q5). After each answer, runs Layer 1 — checks 18 signal fields per trait and marks each as Filled, Partial, Empty, or Negative. Generates targeted probes for empty fields. Max 1 probe per trait. Candidate never knows what signals are being checked.

Prompt B

AI Scorer

Runs after the full transcript is complete. Applies Layer 2 quality descriptors — distinguishes between a Score 5 and Score 4 fill per signal field. Combines both layers using a per-trait scoring matrix built from 100+ recruiter-benchmarked Best/Accepted/Poor samples. Outputs scores, evidence, and holistic notes.

Scoring Scale — Calibrated Against Recruiter Benchmarks
5 — ExceptionalAll signal fields filled at Score 5 quality. Intentional, specific, goes well beyond the expected bar. Example: motivation tied to genuine career pivot, not generic interest.
4 — StrongAll fields filled at Score 4 quality. Clear, honest, professional — meets the standard. Proactive and self-directed, but less intentional than a 5.
3 — AdequateMix of filled and partially filled fields. Generic or scripted. Functional, but signals lack depth. No negative fills.
2 — WeakMostly empty after probing. Vague, disconnected, or minimal effort. No strong evidence for the trait.
1 — PoorNegative fills present. Candidate's answer actively contradicts the trait — blaming, avoidant, or disengaged.
Fast-Track Bar: ≥4.5 average — candidates who clear this threshold are automatically offered a recruiter slot via calendar integration. All others enter the standard review queue.

Holistic Notes

The agency demonstrated in Q5 (test account experimentation, independent resolution) is consistent with the self-direction shown in Q2 (staying late independently to learn the CRM, creating a guide). A pattern of proactive self-sufficiency emerges across both learning and problem-solving contexts.

Integrity Notes

Probe responses in Q3 and Q4 show a register shift — core answers are specific and narrative while some probe responses become more polished and framework-like. This suggests possible rehearsed responses when initial examples are exhausted. Worth pressing harder on probes in subsequent rounds.

Summary

This candidate presents as a motivated, self-directed professional making an intentional transition from hospitality to tech support. Their strongest signal is agency — the Q5 answer demonstrating test-account experimentation and fully independent resolution is genuinely strong. Learning agility and systematic thinking are both solid at score 4. Primary development areas are self-awareness (generic under probing, no gaps acknowledged) and process orientation (tactical under pressure but no evidence of systemic improvement). In the next round, probe deeper on self-reflection and explore whether they think systemically about preventing recurring issues.
